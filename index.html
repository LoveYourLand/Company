Hi, I'm [Your Name] — a forester by training and a native plant enthusiast by passion. I earned my B.S. in Forestry from NC State University, where I developed a deep understanding of plant biology, soil ecology, and how trees and understory plants function together as a system.

What I saw in forestry school stuck with me: the most resilient, beautiful, and low-maintenance landscapes aren't the ones filled with ornamentals from garden centers — they're the ones that reflect what naturally wants to grow here. NC's Piedmont is one of the most biodiverse regions in North America, and the native plants that evolved here are perfectly adapted to our red clay soils, hot summers, and unpredictable winters.

I started Canopy & Root to bring that forestry perspective to residential yards — designing spaces that are beautiful for you and ecologically alive. Whether you want a low-maintenance native border, a full yard transformation, or just want to know why your trees look stressed, I bring science to every visit.

I work directly with every client. When you hire Canopy & Root, you're getting me — not a crew foreman or a subcontractor. I do the assessment, I design the plan, and I'm on-site for the work.

Why it matters

The case for local plants

Native plants aren't just an aesthetic choice — they're an ecological one, with real, measurable benefits for your yard and your neighborhood.

💧

Dramatically less water

Piedmont natives evolved here. After establishment, most thrive on rainfall alone — no irrigation systems, no hand-watering during heat waves.

🐝

Wildlife you can see

Native plants support 29x more specialist bee species than non-native ornamentals. More birds, more butterflies, more fireflies — a yard that feels alive.

🌱

Thrives in our soil

No fighting the Piedmont's heavy red clay. Native plants adapted to it, so you spend less on soil amendments, fertilizer, and replacement plants.

🍂

Year-round interest

Native plant communities are designed by evolution for seasonal change — spring bloom, summer structure, fall color, and winter seedheads that birds depend on.

🌊

Better stormwater

Deep-rooted natives can absorb 10x more water than lawn. They reduce runoff, recharge groundwater, and protect local waterways from nutrient loading.

📉

Lower long-term cost

Less mowing, less fertilizer, less irrigation, fewer replacements. Native landscapes actually cost less to maintain after the first two establishment years.
